## 0. Executive Summary

Five specs, five attacks, five blocks. Zero of the v3.0 squad deliverables survived Round 3b as written. 131 findings, 22 of which are CRITICAL. The concentration of structural (not incidental) failure modes across five independent attack vectors is the single most important signal in this document: the squads were not individually sloppy, they were collectively building on four shared false assumptions that the vision doc never forced them to examine.

The four repeat offenders — documented fully in §2 — are: (1) **LLM-judges-LLM circular evaluation**, where the platform's core claim of "diversity + principled judgment" rests on one Anthropic model grading another Anthropic model and calling the result deterministic; (2) **"sandbox" as prompt-engineering rather than isolation**, where specs name something a sandbox but the actual enforcement is a capability list the model is asked nicely to honor; (3) **persona / variant theater**, where the count of variants (5 critics, 4 adversarial squads, 3 R2 variants, 3 presets, 3 release strategies) is asserted as diversity but no spec measures variant-diversity and every spec's own worked example collapses seats silently; (4) **vendor concentration sold as architecture**, where three third-party control planes (Railway + Cloudflare + Daytona) are named in a spec and the act of naming them is treated as a compliance argument.

A fifth pattern emerged during synthesis: **audit trails generated by the system being audited**. Auto-ADR in 08, HardeningDecisions in 09, composite-score + PartyEvent telemetry in 07 — each pretends to be the evidence layer for the decision layer, but each is produced by the same model family producing the decision. Under EU AI Act scrutiny (Vision §12 residual risk) this is not compliance; it is a liability generator.

Concrete consequences by spec: **05 Custom Agents** ships `run_command` shell access to user-authored YAML with a trust-tier promotion that `winRate > 0` trivially games. **07 Autopilot** ships a hard-cap that isn't hard (5 in-flight candidates × Opus I/O leak $2-10 past the cap on a $5 budget), an FSM missing four real states, and an Aggressive preset that auto-proceeds after 10 minutes of silence through actions the Vision's Non-Negotiable #1 forbids. **08 Asset-Pipeline** ships a content-addressed-storage/GDPR-Article-17 collision, a presigned-URL strip-by-regex that fails on five known obfuscations, and an uncapped Seedance-2 video bleed. **09 Deep-Iterate** ships "5 critics" that are one Haiku re-rolled five times and "3 variants" that converge on one interpolation of the Pragmatic answer the architect was always going to pick. **11 Deployment-Infra** ships "99.9% SLA" without any provider-contractual backing, "region-pinning HARD for GDPR" implemented as a Postgres string column, and "atomic DNS-swap" that is neither atomic nor DNS.

The consolidated recommendation to Nelson is in §6: one HARDEN (08), two REDUCE (05, 09), one DEFER (07), one REDUCE-AS-INTERNAL-ONLY (11). Under this triage, V3.0 ships with Custom-Agents-as-read-only-persona-DSL plus Asset-Pipeline-minus-video plus Deep-Iterate-as-critique-list-only — and Autopilot auto-advance + Deployment-Infra flagship-claims are removed from the vision entirely. Marketing copy that the vision currently depends on ("99.9%", "international-standard", "brief to production-URL in one session", "AI makes the movie while you sleep") must be struck or re-scoped before Round R2 Green-Team spawns. Green-Team's job is to defend what survives, not to rebuild what Red-Team killed.

## 2. Cross-Spec Structural Failures (the repeat offenders)

Each of these patterns appears in three or more of the five attacked specs. They are therefore properties of the v3.0 concept, not defects of any individual squad, and must be addressed globally before any single spec is hardened.

### 2.1 LLM-judges-LLM circular evaluation

**Where it appears:**
- **07 Autopilot** (07-F1, 07-F17): Composite Score formula `AC_fit × 0.6 + Diversity × 0.2 + Cost_fit × 0.2` presents as math but ~60-70% of the score is LLM-on-LLM (`semantic_intent_score` is a Haiku probe; `judge_scores_mean` is Haiku judges; embedding-based Diversity uses another model). 0.85 auto-pick threshold "calibrated on ~200 test races during planning."
- **09 Deep-Iterate** (09-F14): HardeningDecisions table is the spec's compliance artifact — generated by R3's single architect LLM call. The audit trail for the reasoning is produced by the same model family producing the reasoning.
- **08 Asset-Pipeline** (08-F10): Auto-ADR is Sonnet paraphrasing race metadata + committing to git as evidence. User accepts the Sonnet-narrated version, not the race; Evidence points to PartyEvents and loser-branches that may be GC'd.
- **05 Custom Agents** (05-F10, 05-F12): "Four adversarial squads" claim of orthogonality untested; trust-tier promotion on `winRate > 0` uses race-wins as the only signal.
- **09 Deep-Iterate** (09-F01): "5 critics attacking with 5 adjectives" — same model, same weights, same temperature, different labels.

**Why it's structural, not incidental:** PatchParty's core differentiator is "judgment layered on output" — Diversity-Judge, AC-fit score, HardeningDecisions, auto-ADR, composite score. Every layer of judgment uses the same instrument that produced the output being judged. Single-vendor single-family same-weight evaluation does not produce orthogonal signal; it produces shared-blindspot amplification. The whole stack's safety / quality / compliance claims are therefore stacked on a false-diversity assumption.

**Systemic mitigation (global, before any spec hardens):**
- Any "judgment" layer must instrument-diversify: different model family (Anthropic + OpenAI + open-weights), or a deterministic check (AST-diff, schema-check, regex-lint) before the LLM layer.
- Publish pairwise-output-similarity measurements on every multi-LLM layer. Fail-fast at a measured threshold (e.g., cosine > 0.7 on judge outputs → round rejected).
- External test-suite gates where "passes" is objective: `literal_pass` in AC-fit-score is the only defensible half of that term.
- Rename every "audit trail" artifact produced by an LLM to "LLM-authored narrative" or equivalent. Reserve "audit trail" for append-only hash-chained raw-I/O capture (07-F14).
- Any compliance claim (EU AI Act, SOC-2) cites an external auditor, not the spec's own prose.

### 2.2 "Sandbox" is prompt-engineering, not isolation

**Where it appears:**
- **05 Custom Agents** (05-F1, 05-F2): `run_command` shipped with "timeout 60s, no network by default" as the only constraint. No spec of exec vs shell semantics, argv sanitization, ENV scrubbing, cgroup limits, user-ID, cap-drops. The word "sandbox" appears 23 times in the spec; its operational definition appears zero.
- **07 Autopilot** (07-F22, 07-F5): Reversibility-cliff catalogue doesn't list untrusted-agent tool-calls; "pause" is contradicted by "in-flight races allowed to settle" meaning a paused agent's streams continue mutating state.
- **11 Deployment-Infra** (11-F3, 11-F33): Repo-Genesis Saga's "compensating rollback" is pseudocode without rollback-of-rollback semantics; Daytona "sandbox" per project has a 30-minute TTL but no push-to-remote guarantee before commit.
- **09 Deep-Iterate** (09-F08): `<CANDIDATE>` XML tags are the "structural boundary" — SDK-level prompt-engineering, not a security boundary. Attacker includes `</CANDIDATE>` literally; spec does not mention escaping.

**Why it's structural:** The marketing surface ("deepest-in-control", "safety by construction", "hardened against attack") assumes OS-level or network-level isolation at points where the specs deliver a capability list or an XML tag.

**Systemic mitigation:**
- Rename layers honestly. "Sandbox" requires filesystem + network + capability + user-ID + cgroup enforcement documented in a security ADR. Otherwise call it "tool-router" or "capability gate".
- Any claim of "isolation" cites the specific kernel/OS/network primitive (Daytona container config, CF Worker isolate, Postgres RLS, R2 IAM) — not the DSL construct that merely passes intent to the model.
- Prompt-injection defenses require a meta-reviewer in a different trust domain (different model family, different API key, different network path), not four rhetorical layers in one prompt.

### 2.3 Persona / Variant Theater

**Where it appears:**
- **09 Deep-Iterate** (09-F01, 09-F02, 09-F16): 5 critics = 1 model × 5 adjective labels; 3 R2 variants collapse to one interpolation; spec's own force-diversity rule exists because variants converge by design.
- **05 Custom Agents** (05-F12, 05-F16): 4 pre-baked adversarial squads all route through the same tool-router with the same model default — no measured IoU on finding-sets; Sven's elaborate persona produces <5% AST-diff from baseline on non-UI non-auth stories (prediction; no measurement in spec).
- **07 Autopilot** (07-F4): 3 presets (Conservative / Balanced / Aggressive) share identical `never_auto` lists covering 60%+ of real cliffs; differentiation is purely constants, not behavior.
- **11 Deployment-Infra** (11-F29): 3 release strategies (Canary / Blue-Green / Big-Bang) rejected alternatives on "DX grounds" — DX is not a variant-axis.
- Vision §5 itself: "5 race personas" referenced throughout — no orthogonal-output measurement anywhere.

**Why it's structural:** Variant count is asserted ("5 critics", "4 squads", "3 presets") but variant-diversity is never measured. The entire value proposition of the race-mechanic is diversity; nowhere in any spec is pairwise output-diversity plotted or gated.

**Systemic mitigation:**
- Every "N variants" feature ships with a pairwise-diversity measurement: AST-diff for code, semantic cosine for text, finding-set IoU for adversarial outputs.
- Force-re-roll below measured threshold, with budget-aware cap on re-rolls.
- Published per-variant selection-rate data before claiming variants are meaningfully different. If Strategic gets 0% R3 selection (09-F18), Strategic is decoration.
- Personas must ship with behavioral-delta eval: same input → persona-A vs persona-B → measured output-distance across N story archetypes. If <5%, persona is cosmetic-only and flagged in UI.

### 2.4 Vendor-concentration-as-architecture

**Where it appears:**
- **11 Deployment-Infra** (11-F1, 11-F7, 11-F15, 11-F32): Three-provider stack (Railway + CF + Daytona) composed-availability is 99.7% not 99.9%; Railway's GraphQL control-plane is undocumented; CF Worker tenancy model unspecified; no fault-tree.
- **08 Asset-Pipeline** (08-F15, 08-F1): Single vendor per asset type (GPT-image-1, Recraft, Seedance-2, Sonnet). "Fallback chain" is single-hop to another single vendor with no SLO, no circuit-breaker, no degraded-mode queue. Seedance had three >2h outages in 2025-Q4 per the spec's own citation.
- **08 Asset-Pipeline** (08-F20): R2 "zero egress" claim scoped only to CF-network consumers, not universally true.
- **07 Autopilot** (07-F7): Brownfield Autopilot's GitHub API rate-limit is shared with live human activity; self-DDOS on user's repo is by design.
- **11 Deployment-Infra** (11-F10): `europe-west4` labeled "GDPR-safe" with no DPA, SCCs, TIAs, or RoPA. Region identifier treated as compliance proof.

**Why it's structural:** Every spec names providers and treats naming as architecture. None defines a provider-abstraction layer, none specifies a fault-tree, none computes composed availability, none negotiates contractual SLAs.

**Systemic mitigation:**
- Require a provider-abstraction interface for every external dep (image gen, video gen, LLM, infra control-plane) before any resilience claim.
- Publish composed-availability math and a degradation-mode matrix: what still works when each provider is down?
- Circuit-breakers + per-vendor budget + user-visible provider-status dashboard for every asset type and every infra layer.
- "SLA" and "compliance" claims require contracts, not spec prose.

### 2.5 Audit-trail-by-self-report

**Where it appears:**
- **07 Autopilot** (07-F8, 07-F14, 07-F28): PartyEvent stream is the audit trail. No raw-LLM-I/O capture; no append-only enforcement; no hash-chain; `AutopilotCheckpoint.snapshotHash` is a feature-in-spec-only with no verification system wired. Agent can delete its own "spec.gaming_detected" events.
- **08 Asset-Pipeline** (08-F10, 08-F23): Auto-ADR is Sonnet-authored narrative committed to git as evidence. Evidence cites PartyEvents + loser-branches + prior ADRs — each individually GC'able, mutable, or rebase-able. No cryptographic pinning, no content-hashing.
- **09 Deep-Iterate** (09-F14): HardeningDecisions table is R3 LLM self-report; `confidence` field is ornamental (never calibrated); no deterministic cross-check between `decision.flawId` and `finalSpec.body`.
- **11 Deployment-Infra** (11-F10, 11-F14): Compliance is a 10-item hygiene checklist labeled "international standard"; DPA/SCCs/TIAs/RoPA absent.

**Why it's structural:** EU AI Act is Vision §12 existential risk. The Vision's own defense ("transparency-by-construction") requires tamper-evident, non-repudiable, LLM-I/O-complete logs. None of the squads built that; each built a Prisma table and called it audit.

**Systemic mitigation:**
- Append-only PartyEvent enforced at DB trigger (reject DELETE/UPDATE).
- Hash-chain each event over prev-hash + event-body.
- Raw LLM I/O (prompt + completion + tool args + tool result) persisted as content-addressed blob, referenced from PartyEvent.
- Background cold-export to a separate trust boundary (R2 or equivalent) with reconciliation alert on hash-chain break.
- "Audit-trail" naming is reserved for this layer. LLM-authored narrative is labeled as such everywhere it surfaces.

### 2.6 Economics-asserted-not-modeled

**Where it appears:**
- **07 Autopilot** (07-F3, 07-F11, 07-F16, 07-F27): Phase allocation 5/10/5/10/5/50/10/5 asserted as scale-invariant with no math; Shoestring $5 realistic actual is $15+ on impl; judging/probe/diversity costs not in any phase allocation; hard-cap leaks in-flight.
- **08 Asset-Pipeline** (08-F4, 08-F7): Costs priced at 2026-04 model prices with no versioning mechanism; Seedance fleet-cost uncapped; quarterly review is a human process.
- **09 Deep-Iterate** (09-F04, 09-F12): "Hard envelope $0.75/$3/$8" contradicted by spec's own `overrun: true` at 130-200%; Autopilot Conservative mandates 4× $8 Intensive = $32 hardening floor before race costs.
- **11 Deployment-Infra** (11-F21): Railway tier table ($5/$20/$50) excludes Postgres Pro, backup, egress, volume — real cost 2-3× stated.

**Why it's structural:** Vision §5 Principle #7 (Budget-Governor) and §13 Non-Negotiable #1 ("user must not wake up to a $400 bill") are marketing promises backed by guess-work. Every economics claim in every spec is an asserted ratio or an asserted envelope with no empirical grounding and no version-over-time model.

**Systemic mitigation:**
- Provider pricing in a versioned config table (`ProviderPricing`), weekly cron-refreshed, audit-trailed per cost-ledger row.
- Pre-flight cost estimator on every budget-relevant action; refuse > 110% of stated envelope.
- Hard-cap must cancel in-flight (not drain); document the irreducible 1-5% leak.
- Real-data-before-release: every preset re-calibrated against at least 100 actual races with published empirical distribution before shipping paid tier.
